## Part A — Strategic Context · *Why this exists*

### 1 · Problem & Opportunity

- **Problem statement:** I'm a B1/B2 Spanish learner and a heavy reader of classic literature. My study time is unscaffolded: Spanish lessons happen (recorded, ~1 hour, with a tutor) but nothing structures the time between them; workbook pages from my tutor have no follow-up system; and the vocabulary I meet in books goes into a separate flashcard app (Mochi) only if I manually type it in. The result is too much time scrolling and too little time learning. I need one place that ingests the material I'm actually studying (workbook scans, lesson recordings, Spanish texts, English classics), turns it into spaced-repetition study material automatically, and teaches me the grammar I'm missing.
- **Why now:** AI makes it possible to build a personal, special-purpose super-teacher — extraction, transcription, definitions, lesson generation, and grading are now commodity capabilities. There is no reason to wait for a product company to build this for me.
- **Why us:** Because I want to learn and want to break out of the scroll lock. I am the user; motivation and requirements live in the same head.

### 2 · Audience & Users

- **Primary persona(s):** The owner. An adult, self-directed learner: B1/B2 Spanish aiming for C1, and an avid reader who wants to read the classics — the near-term reading goal is the **King James Bible** — without a dictionary. Studies **daily**, often from the phone. Frustration: study material is scattered across lesson recordings, workbooks, novels, and a flashcard app that requires manual data entry.
- **Secondary users & stakeholders:** Friends of the owner who might borrow the app casually. They get no accounts, no personalization, no support guarantees.
- **Anti-personas — who it is NOT for:** Anyone who wants passive content. The app demands answers — it quizzes, it grades, it schedules reviews. There is nothing to open and merely watch. It is also not for the general public: no growth, no onboarding for strangers, no scale.

### ★ 3 · Vision, Goals & Non-Goals

- **One-line vision:** Turn my phone and laptop into an AI-assisted super-teacher for the subjects I actually care about — starting with Spanish, then English literary vocabulary.
- **Goals (measurable outcomes):**
  - [ ] Spanish solidly at C1: sustain ≥85% accuracy on C1-level vocabulary and grammar quizzes generated by the app over a rolling 2-week window. *(Stretch: read Cervantes.)*
  - [ ] English literary vocabulary: master (per the SRS algorithm's "mature" state) the above-threshold word list of the **King James Bible**, so it can be read without lookups. *(Other classics — Melville, Hawthorne, Dickens — follow the same pipeline later.)*
  - [ ] The app is actually used: owner completes review sessions on at least 5 of the first 7 days after Phase 1 ships, **on either phone or desktop browser** — daily study is the owner's stated rhythm.
- **Non-goals (explicitly out of scope):**
  - No feeds, no passive consumption, no engagement mechanics (streak pressure, badges, mascots, notifications designed to hook).
  - Single user. No accounts system, no multi-tenancy, no building for scale or for millions of users.
  - **No native mobile app** — but the *mobile browser is a primary surface from Phase 1* (owner revision, 2026-06-10). Review, triage, and quiz flows must be thumb-friendly from the start. "No native app" remains a hard non-goal; if a feature is materially harder in a web app than it would be natively (e.g., system-wide text selection capture), simplify or skip it rather than going native.
  - No TTS / audio playback of words in v1 — pronunciation audio is logged in `TODO-LATER.md` (see §6.10), not built. *(Audio **input** — lesson recordings, voice questions — IS in scope; only audio output is deferred.)*
  - No accessibility audit or WCAG compliance work (basic readability and tap-target sanity only — note tradeoffs per §7).
  - No hardened security posture (see §7 — note tradeoffs and move on).
  - No offline mode. Phone use assumes the server is reachable (owner will run an always-on machine — §8).
  - No translation-exercise grading in v1 (owner: "I don't need to translate right now"). Text ingestion finds unknown vocabulary; it does not grade paragraph translations.
  - No additional subjects (theater, music, art events) in v1 — the data model may leave room (a `subject` dimension), but zero UI or features are built for them.
  - No SMS/email word-capture inbox in v1. The owner's long-term wish — "text a phone number a word and an agent adds it" — is recorded in `TODO-LATER.md` as a future inbox channel; nothing in v1 builds toward it beyond keeping word-creation available behind a clean internal API.

## Part B — Product Definition · *What it is*

### 4 · Product Description

- **Elevator pitch:** Use your phone to learn what's interesting to you — not in a Duolingo hook-you way, but in a real, spaced-repetition, useful way. Feed it your Spanish workbook scans, your recorded tutor lessons, Spanish texts, and classic English literature; it extracts the words you don't know (including the ones you flagged out loud — *"esta palabra, no sé"*), defines them, schedules them for review, quizzes you in both directions, teaches you the grammar you're missing, grades you, explains why, and answers your questions in context. It runs in your laptop browser and your phone's browser, served from an always-on machine you control.
- **Core capabilities:**
  1. **Ingest** — scanned Spanish workbook PDFs, pasted Spanish/English text, **recorded tutor lessons (audio)**, Project Gutenberg books (by URL/ID — the KJV is the first target), Mochi exports, and manual entry → extraction of above-threshold vocabulary with auto-filled definitions → human triage (know it / learn it / skip).
  2. **Review** — a spaced-repetition engine (SM-2) that schedules every kept word and quizzes both directions (word→definition, definition→word), with a manual "I forgot this — resurface it" override.
  3. **Quiz** — generated multiple-choice questions, including LLM-generated sentences that test vocabulary in context; deterministic grading; results feed the SRS history; mistakes boost the missed word's review priority.
  4. **Grammar** — an AI-built curriculum of Spanish grammar topics in distinct categories (tenses, semantic contrasts like *ya/todavía* and *por/para*, etc.), generated lessons (explanation → examples → quiz), LLM grading of free-text answers, "explain why" on any question, and a mastery-weighted suggested-practice queue.
  5. **Ask** — a persistent, page-context-aware chatbot available on most pages via a plain "Ask" button (no floating bubble), able to answer language questions and add words to decks mid-conversation.
  6. **Listen** — lesson-recording analysis (flagged words, tutor corrections, struggle sentences, topics covered) and voice questions (record a question, get an answer, relevant items enter SRS).
  7. **Suggest** — individual new-word and grammar-topic suggestions calibrated to the owner's level, one at a time, add/skip, never repeating what's already been offered.
  8. **Manage** — full control of the word library: add, edit, delete, search, organize by deck/subject.
- **What it is NOT:** Not Duolingo (no gamified engagement loop). Not a general-purpose Anki/Mochi replacement for arbitrary decks — it is purpose-built for this owner's two subjects. Not an e-reader. Not a content feed. Not a SaaS product. Not a translation grader (v1).
- **What it runs on:** A web app served by a single small server on an always-on machine the owner controls. **Phone browser and desktop browser are co-primary surfaces from Phase 1.** One process, one SQLite file on local disk (locations documented — see §6.8).

### ★ 5 · User Stories & Acceptance Criteria

**Phasing (owner mandate, re-ranked 2026-06-10):**
- **Phase 1 — Spanish core:** PDF ingestion → SRS review loop → raw text ingestion → quizzes → grammar lessons/quizzes. Mobile-responsive from the first screen.
- **Phase 2 — Spanish AI layer:** lesson-recording ingestion → Ask chatbot → individual word/grammar suggestions → voice questions.
- **Phase 3 — English:** Gutenberg/KJV ingestion → English-level calibration (+ optional placement assessment) → Mochi import (fixtures permitting).
- **Phase 4 — Polish:** guidance surfacing, progress view, backup/export polish, word-select-to-add (if cheap), `TODO-LATER.md` items reconsidered.

MoSCoW priorities below apply within the initiative. Build in phase order; within any slice, riskiest part first.

- **[Must · Phase 1 · build first] Spanish PDF ingestion.** As the owner, I want to upload a scanned page (or multi-page PDF) from a Spanish workbook so that words above my level become study material without manual typing.
  - *Given* a scanned Spanish workbook PDF, *when* I upload it, *then* an async job extracts candidate words above the B1/B2 threshold, each with definition, example sentence, part of speech, and estimated CEFR level, and presents them in a triage list (know / learn / skip); words marked "learn" appear in the Spanish deck with definitions filled and enter the SRS queue. The original PDF is stored and linked as the source.
  - *Additionally*, when a scanned page contains a **grammar explanation** (vs. vocabulary/exercises), the page is tagged as grammar material and linked to the curriculum (§6.5) so lessons and quizzes can be generated aligned to what the tutor is actually teaching.
- **[Must · Phase 1] Daily review with spaced repetition.** As the owner, I want to open the app — usually on my phone — and review what's due so that I actually retain words.
  - *Given* cards due today, *when* I open Review, *then* I'm quizzed one card at a time (multiple-choice by default, flip-card fallback), in a randomized direction (word→definition or definition→word); each card shows the term **as encountered and its lemma** plus part of speech; the answer reveal shows **both** the Spanish monolingual definition and the English gloss (display preference is a setting, default: both); *when* I answer, *then* correctness is recorded with grades **didn't know / knew it / easy** (mapped to SM-2 quality scores — "easy" is standard SM-2 practice and lengthens the interval), the schedule updates next-due, and *when* I close and reopen the app (or the server restarts), *then* no state is lost.
  - *Manual resurface override:* *given* the library list, *when* I mark a word "I forgot this," *then* its card becomes due immediately and its SM-2 state is demoted (interval reset, ease reduced one step), overriding whatever the algorithm believed.
- **[Must · Phase 1] Raw text ingestion.** As the owner, I want to paste raw Spanish (or English) text and get the same extraction → triage → deck pipeline.
  - *Given* pasted text, *when* I submit it, *then* the identical triage flow runs and kept words enter the appropriate deck with source recorded.
- **[Must · Phase 1] Multiple-choice quizzes, both directions.** As the owner, I want generated MC quizzes — including random sentences that test a word in context — so that recognition is tested actively.
  - *Given* a deck with ≥4 words, *when* I start a quiz (choosing deck, length — **default 10 questions** — direction or mixed), *then* each question has one correct answer and 3 plausible distractors drawn from the same deck (or LLM-generated for sentence/cloze questions), grading is immediate, a wrong answer shows a **pre-generated, cached explanation** of why the correct answer is right and mine is wrong, results are saved, and **every miss boosts that word in SRS** (card due now, interval reset per SM-2 failure).
- **[Must · Phase 1] Spanish grammar lessons and quizzes.** As the owner, I want the app to generate grammar lessons and quiz me on them so that grammar keeps pace with vocabulary.
  - *Given* the AI-seeded curriculum (distinct categories — see §6.5), *when* I pick a topic, *then* the session flows **explanation → examples → quiz**; *when* I take the quiz, *then* MC/conjugation/fill-in answers are graded (free text via LLM), each question offers "explain why" (including on correct answers), topic mastery is tracked, and **low-mastery topics surface sooner in a suggested-practice queue**.
- **[Must · Phase 1] Library management.** As the owner, I want to add, edit, and delete words directly.
  - *Given* the library view, *when* I add a word manually, *then* its definition, part of speech, and lemma are auto-filled (editable) and it enters the SRS queue; *when* I delete a word, *then* it and its schedule disappear (review history retained, flagged orphaned); the "I forgot this" resurface action is available on every row.
- **[Must · Phase 2] Lesson-recording ingestion.** As the owner, I record my ~1-hour tutor lessons (Google Meet; audio-only is fine) and want the app to mine them.
  - *Given* an uploaded audio file (up to ~60 min; common formats: m4a/mp3/ogg/wav), *when* the job runs, *then* the audio is transcribed via the transcription provider (§6.7b), the transcript is stored with the Source, and the LLM extracts: (a) **words I flagged as unknown** (e.g., *"esta palabra, no sé"* and similar self-flags), (b) **corrections my tutor made** (my version vs. corrected version), (c) **sentences I struggled to produce** (long pauses, restarts, tutor rescues — best-effort from transcript), and (d) **grammar topics covered**; flagged words flow into the standard triage pipeline; corrections and struggle sentences are stored as reviewable LessonInsights linked to relevant grammar topics; covered topics update the curriculum's "seen in lessons" record.
- **[Must · Phase 2] Ask chatbot.** As the owner, I want a plain "Ask" button on most pages (no floating bubble) opening a chat that knows what I'm looking at.
  - *Given* I'm viewing a word, lesson, quiz question, or review card, *when* I open Ask, *then* the chat is seeded with that page context; chats **persist** as threads (the AI has memory of prior conversations); *when* the conversation surfaces a word worth learning, *then* the assistant can **add it to a deck via tool call** with my confirmation shown inline; all chat turns go through the server-side LLM layer and are logged like any other call.
- **[Must · Phase 2] Individual word & grammar suggestions.** As the owner, I want the app to proactively suggest things I likely don't know.
  - *Given* my known/learning/mastered words and grammar mastery, *when* I open Suggestions, *then* the app proposes **individual words** (Spanish or English) and **grammar topics**, one at a time, each with definition/preview; actions = add / skip; every suggestion is recorded so **nothing is ever re-suggested**; suggestions are calibrated against my actual level (the calibration sample of §6.1) so I'm not shown words I obviously know.
- **[Must · Phase 2] Voice questions.** As the owner, I want to record myself asking a question — "how do I say X?", "I heard someone say Y, what does it mean?" — and get an answer.
  - *Given* a recorded clip (browser MediaRecorder upload), *when* I submit it, *then* it's transcribed, the question is answered in a persistent Ask thread, and any words/structures central to the answer are offered as one-tap additions to SRS.
- **[Must · Phase 3] Project Gutenberg ingestion — first corpus: the King James Bible.** As the owner, I want to point the app at a Gutenberg book so that I'm eventually tested on all of its hard words.
  - *Given* a Gutenberg URL or ID, *when* I submit it, *then* the plain text is fetched, unique words are LLM-filtered per the college-student rubric (§6.1) — with **explicit handling of archaic forms**: archaic function words and spellings (*thee, thou, doth, unto, saith*) are excluded as noise, while genuinely hard archaic/literary vocabulary (*concupiscence, propitiation, firmament*) is kept — each carrying a likely-known estimate calibrated on my learned words, surfaced in triage batches (~50) with auto-filled definitions; kept words enter the English deck; a coverage indicator shows triage progress and untested-kept-word count. Every kept word eventually enters the review rotation automatically.
- **[Must · Phase 3] English level calibration.** As the owner, I want suggestions and filtering to know my (strong) English vocabulary level.
  - *Given* my known/mastered English words, *then* calibration uses them as examples in every classification batch; *additionally*, an **optional one-time placement-style assessment** (a short adaptive vocab quiz) is available to seed the level estimate before much history exists. Both mechanisms exist; neither is mandatory.
- **[Could · Phase 3] Mochi import.** As the owner, I want to import my existing ~300-card Mochi vocabulary so history isn't thrown away.
  - *Given* a Mochi export file in the owner's format (**fixture files required before this is built — see §17**), *when* I import it, *then* cards become words in the right deck, duplicates are detected and merged or skipped with a report.
- **[Should · Phase 4] Word-select-to-add.** Within app-rendered text (definitions, lessons, examples, chat replies), selecting/long-pressing a word offers "add to Spanish/English deck," enabling cascade learning (a word inside another word's Spanish definition). **Escape hatch (owner-approved):** this is a web app; if selection UX proves disproportionately hard on mobile browsers, ship a reduced version (tappable words in app-generated content only) or skip with a `DECISIONS.md` entry — do not reach for a native app.
- **[Should · Phase 4] Progress view.** Counts by status (new/learning/mature), due forecast, quiz accuracy trends, per-book coverage, grammar mastery heatmap.
- **[Should · Phase 4] Backup & export.** One-click JSON export and automatic local DB backups (the automatic backups themselves are Phase 1 — §6.8; this story is the polished UI).
- **[Could] Per-word notes and multiple senses.** Attach personal notes or additional senses to a word.
- **[Won't (v1)]** Native apps, TTS/audio playback (→ `TODO-LATER.md`), SMS/email capture inbox (→ `TODO-LATER.md`), translation grading, additional subject tabs, social features.

### ★ 6 · Functional Requirements

#### Feature area 1: Ingestion & word capture
- Accept PDF uploads (scans). Extraction pipeline uses the vision-capable LLM (see §8) to read scanned pages directly — no separate OCR step. Per-page processing with progress UI; failed pages retryable individually. Pages classified as **vocabulary/exercise** vs. **grammar explanation**; grammar pages link into the curriculum (§6.5).
- Accept pasted raw text (Spanish or English; language selected or auto-detected).
- Accept **lesson audio uploads** (Phase 2): m4a/mp3/ogg/wav, up to ~60 min. Pipeline: chunked transcription via the transcription provider (§6.7b) → transcript stored on the Source → LLM analysis pass extracting flagged-unknown words, tutor corrections (original + corrected pair), struggle sentences, and grammar topics covered. Flagged words join the standard triage flow; the rest are stored as `LessonInsight` records browsable per lesson and per grammar topic. Upfront cost estimate shown before processing (transcription + analysis).
- Accept **voice questions** (Phase 2): short browser recordings → transcription → answered in a persistent Ask thread → one-tap add of relevant items to SRS.
- Fetch Project Gutenberg books by URL or ID (plain-text format). Store the raw text with the Source record. First target corpus: the King James Bible.
- **Word filtering — Spanish:** candidate words are those the LLM classifies above the owner's B1/B2 baseline (CEFR estimate stored per word; owner can override).
- **Word filtering — English (the owner's definition of "test me on all the words"):** unique words from the source are sent to the LLM in batches; it flags every word that is **advanced, or that a reasonable, intelligent college student wouldn't know**. That sentence is the rubric — keep it near-verbatim in the versioned prompt template. For archaic texts (KJV), the rubric is extended: exclude archaic *function* words and mere spelling variants; include genuinely difficult archaic/literary vocabulary. A cheap local pre-pass (function words / very-high-frequency stopwords, proper nouns) exists only to cut token cost; it is never the semantic filter.
- **Likely-known calibration (both languages):** every classification batch includes a sample of the owner's known and mastered words as calibration examples; the LLM returns a per-word *likely-known* estimate alongside the difficulty call. Predictions assist triage (grouping, sorting, bulk actions) — they never silently drop a word. An **optional placement assessment** (short adaptive vocab quiz, English first) can seed the level estimate before review history accumulates.
- Triage UI: batched (~50), each item shows word, definition, part of speech, example, level estimate, and the likely-known prediction; items grouped by prediction ("probably new" / "you may know these") with per-group bulk confirm; actions = **know** / **learn** / **skip**. **Thumb-operable on phones and keyboard-operable on desktop from Phase 1** (both are primary surfaces).
- Definitions auto-filled at ingestion: Spanish words store a Spanish (monolingual) definition **and** an English gloss plus a Spanish example sentence; English words store an English definition plus the source sentence when available. Answer reveals show **both definitions by default**; display preference is a setting the owner may change after living with it.
- Every word stores **part of speech** and both the **encountered form and the lemma**; cards display both (e.g., *tuviera — tener*).
- Dedupe on ingestion against existing words (case/accent-normalized lemma match); duplicates surfaced, never silently dropped.
- Mochi importer (Phase 3 — fixtures required): isolated behind a parser interface so format corrections are cheap. ~300 existing cards expected.
- All ingestion writes a Source record (type: pdf | text | lesson_audio | voice_question | gutenberg | mochi | manual | chat | suggestion; title; ref/URL; stored original).

#### Feature area 2: Vocabulary database & library
- Words: term (as encountered), lemma, language, **part of speech**, definitions (es/en as applicable), example sentence, CEFR/difficulty estimate, status (new | learning | mature | known | suspended), deck/subject, source link, timestamps.
- Full CRUD; search (accent-insensitive); filter by deck, status, source; bulk actions (delete, suspend).
- **"I forgot this" action** on any word: card becomes due immediately; SM-2 interval reset and ease demoted one step; logged in ReviewLog as a manual demotion.
- Decks are per language/subject; v1 ships two: Spanish, English Vocabulary. Schema allows more; UI does not invent them.

#### Feature area 3: Spaced repetition engine
- Algorithm: **SM-2** (classic, ~30 lines, well documented). Implement in-house with unit tests; do not pull a library.
- Each word is one card. Each review randomly selects direction (word→definition or definition→word); per-direction correctness is logged.
- Review item rendering: multiple-choice by default (distractors from same deck; LLM-generated cloze sentences mixed in, served from cache); flip-card self-grade fallback when the deck has <4 cards or no cached question exists.
- Grading inputs: **didn't know / knew it / easy** — three grades, mapped to SM-2 quality scores (≈2 / 4 / 5). "Easy" is standard SM-2 and is included (owner-confirmed).
- Quiz mistakes anywhere in the app (deck quizzes, grammar cloze with a vocab word) write a failure into that word's review history and pull its due date to now.
- Track every review in an append-only ReviewLog (including manual "forgot this" demotions) so the schedule is recomputable if the algorithm ever has a bug.
- New cards enter the queue automatically (configurable new-cards/day, **default 20** — a typical lesson yields ~30 new words, so the queue drains in ~1.5 days of daily study) — no manual activation.

#### Feature area 4: Quiz generation & grading
- On-demand quizzes: choose deck, length (**default 10**), question style (definition-match MC, sentence/cloze MC, mixed — default mixed), direction (word→def, def→word, mixed — default mixed). Vocabulary quizzes and grammar quizzes are **separate** in v1; a combined mode is a `TODO-LATER.md` candidate.
- LLM generates sentence/cloze questions **with the explanation generated and cached at the same time** (owner delegated; rationale: marginal generation cost is trivial, and cached explanations make wrong-answer feedback instant and consistent — `DECISIONS.md`).
- All generated questions are cached in the DB and reused before regenerating.
- Deterministic grading for MC; immediate per-question feedback; wrong answers show the cached explanation; quiz results stored and written into each word's review history (misses boost — §6.3).
- Distractor quality rule: same language, similar level, never the correct answer's synonym; flag-question button stores bad questions for exclusion.

#### Feature area 5: Grammar study (Spanish)
- **Curriculum seeding (owner-delegated):** the curriculum is generated by prompting the strongest available model *as a tutor teaching Spanish as a second language* to lay out the topics a B1→C1 student must master, organized into **distinct categories** — at minimum: tenses & moods; semantic contrasts (*ya/todavía*, *por/para*, *ser/estar*, etc.); pronouns & clitics; clauses & connectors; prepositions & idiomatic usage. The generated curriculum is stored, editable, and extendable; the seeding prompt lives in `/prompts` and is versioned.
- **Tutor-material alignment:** grammar pages from uploaded workbook scans (§6.1) and topics detected in lesson recordings link to curriculum topics, guiding which lessons to generate next so the app teaches what the tutor is actually covering.
- Lesson generator: topic + owner level → structured lesson with the fixed flow **explanation → examples (es + en) → quiz**. Cached; regenerate only on request.
- Lesson quizzes: MC, fill-in-the-blank, conjugation drills, short free-text. Free-text graded by LLM with a one-line verdict + explanation; MC graded deterministically.
- "Explain why" available on every question, including ones answered correctly.
- **Mastery & practice queue:** topic mastery tracked from quiz history; topics with low scores resurface sooner in a suggested-practice queue shown on the grammar home screen (loose spaced repetition at the topic level — owner-confirmed model).

#### Feature area 6: Suggestions & curriculum guidance
- **Individual suggestions (its own feature, Phase 2):** a Suggestions surface proposes one item at a time — a Spanish word, an English word, or a grammar topic — selected by the LLM using the owner's known/learning/mastered words, grammar mastery, and the likely-known calibration. Actions: **add** (word → deck + SRS; topic → practice queue) or **skip**. Every proposal is persisted in a `Suggestion` table so the same item is **never suggested twice** (skips included). English suggestions must respect the owner's strong baseline vocabulary — err toward harder words.
- **"What next" guidance (Phase 4 polish):** when due reviews are low and untriaged content is exhausted, surface proposed grammar topics (from mastery gaps) and vocabulary themes. Owner accepts or dismisses. Never auto-adds without acceptance.

#### Feature area 7: LLM integration layer
- **Hot-swappable provider (owner mandate).** All AI flows through a thin `LlmProvider` interface (`complete()`, `vision()`, normalized usage/cost reporting) with one adapter per provider. Ship the Anthropic adapter; switching the active provider or model must be a config change only (env/Settings: provider, model, key) — adding a brand-new provider means writing exactly one adapter file. No provider-specific types, options, or prompt syntax may leak outside the adapters.
- All AI calls go through the server (never from the browser) via that single internal module, with task-specific, provider-neutral prompt templates kept in versioned files.
- **Chat with tools:** the Ask feature runs through the same layer with a small server-defined tool set (initially: `add_word_to_deck`, `lookup_word`, `get_page_context`). Tool calls that mutate data render an inline confirmation in the chat UI before committing.
- **Cache aggressively:** every generated artifact (definition, lesson, quiz question, explanation, curriculum, suggestion batch) is persisted and reused. Never regenerate what is stored.
- Log every call: task, model, tokens in/out, latency, cache hit/miss, cost estimate. Surface cumulative spend in the System page.
- Failures retry with backoff; user input is never lost on LLM failure (jobs persist and are resumable).

#### Feature area 7b: Transcription layer (Phase 2)
- **Second pre-approved paid dependency (owner-confirmed):** a speech-to-text API for lesson recordings and voice questions. Default: OpenAI Whisper API (or equally capable successor); wrapped in a `TranscriptionProvider` adapter mirroring the `LlmProvider` pattern — hot-swappable, server-side only, key in `.env`.
- Long recordings are chunked (provider limits), transcribed as resumable job steps, and stitched; per-lesson cost estimate (~$0.40 for an hour at current Whisper pricing) shown before processing and logged to the spend page.
- Spanish/English code-switching is expected in lessons; configure transcription accordingly (no forced single language).

#### Feature area 8: Persistence, backup, jobs
- SQLite (WAL mode) as the single source of truth; survives restarts. All on-disk state lives under one configurable data directory: the DB file, timestamped backups, uploaded originals (PDFs, audio), and fetched book texts. The README carries a **"Where your data lives"** section documenting these paths, backup cadence, and the copy-to-restore procedure. *(SQLite needs no install or server process — the database is just a file on the local machine.)*
- Automatic timestamped backup copy of the DB file (daily and before every schema migration); manual JSON export/import of all user data.
- Long-running work (PDF pages, lesson transcription/analysis, book processing) runs as persistent server-side jobs with status visible in the UI; jobs survive restarts and resume from the last completed chunk. The host is an **always-on machine** (owner-confirmed), so overnight book-scale jobs are acceptable.

#### Feature area 9: System & observability (agents build this — it matters)
- Structured JSON server logs (request, job, LLM-call, transcription-call, error-with-stack).
- In-app **System** page: recent errors, job statuses, LLM + transcription spend to date, DB/backup status.
- Repo carries `DECISIONS.md`: every tradeoff taken for/against security, performance, reliability, accessibility, or observability is logged there with one-line rationale (standing instruction to all agents).

#### Feature area 10: Deferred-wishes ledger
- Repo carries `TODO-LATER.md` — owner-acknowledged wishes explicitly cut from v1, so they aren't lost: **TTS/audio pronunciation playback**; **SMS/email word-capture inbox ("text the app a word")**; combined vocab+grammar quizzes; additional subjects; full word-select-to-add if the reduced version ships instead.

### 7 · Non-Functional Requirements

- **Performance:** No targets; unmeasured. Be reasonably responsive; anything >2s (ingestion, transcription, generation) must run async with progress and never block the UI. Cache-first LLM usage is the main performance lever.
- **Reliability:** The one hard requirement: **the database persists.** No data loss across restarts, deploys, or crashes. Automatic backups (§6.8); restore procedure documented and exercised once before release done.
- **Security:** Explicitly an afterthought. Server-side-only API keys (LLM + transcription); `.env` never committed; if the server is ever exposed beyond LAN/VPN, a single shared-secret login gate is the maximum v1 measure. Every shortcut gets a line in `DECISIONS.md`. Do not build accounts, roles, or auth flows.
- **Accessibility:** Not a goal. Maintain only baseline usability: readable type sizes, sane contrast, **≥44px tap targets (now load-bearing — phone is a primary surface)**, visible keyboard focus on desktop. Skipped work is noted in `DECISIONS.md`.
- **Observability/logging:** First-class (see §6.9) because AI agents build and maintain this codebase: logs, the System page, and `DECISIONS.md` are how agents and the human stay oriented. Code is written to be read by AIs and used by a human: clear module boundaries, descriptive names, README that lets a fresh agent orient in one read.

### 16 · Risks, Edge Cases & Failure Modes

- **Top risks:**
  - Risk: Vision extraction misreads poor scans. — Mitigation: per-page processing with editable triage output, per-page retry, store the original; validate on a real workbook scan early (§11 slice 1).
  - Risk: Lesson-transcript mining misses spoken flags ("no sé" said many ways) or hallucinates corrections. — Mitigation: extraction prompt lists the owner's real flagging phrases (collect from the first real transcript and version them); everything mined lands in human triage / browsable insights, never silently into the deck; transcript stored verbatim so re-analysis is free.
  - Risk: Transcription cost/quality on hour-long, code-switching audio. — Mitigation: chunked resumable jobs, upfront per-lesson estimate, provider adapter makes swapping cheap, spend page + $25 ceiling.
  - Risk: LLM cost creep on book-scale ingestion. — Mitigation: upfront cost estimate + chunked confirmation, aggressive caching, spend page, $-ceiling escalation (§13).
  - Risk: Difficulty misclassification floods triage with noise — or likely-known predictions go wrong; KJV's archaic English confuses the rubric. — Mitigation: triage *is* the human check; predictions only group/sort, never drop; rubric + archaic-handling + calibration sample live in versioned prompt templates; owner-overridable level per word; flag button feeds exclusions.
  - Risk: SRS bug corrupts scheduling. — Mitigation: exhaustive unit tests on SM-2 (including manual demotion); append-only ReviewLog makes every schedule recomputable.
  - Risk: Always-on machine fails. — Mitigation: automatic timestamped backups, documented restore, DB file trivially copyable.
  - Risk: Scope creep. — Mitigation: §3 non-goals + `TODO-LATER.md` gives wishes a home that isn't the codebase; orchestrator checks non-goals every loop.
- **Edge cases that must be handled:**
  - Duplicate words across sources (merge/skip with report, never silent) — including a lesson-flagged word that already exists from a PDF.
  - Accent/case normalization (`más` vs `mas`, `ñ`); multi-word expressions ("por lo tanto") are first-class terms; encountered-form vs. lemma both stored and displayed.
  - Words with multiple senses: one card per term+language; definitions editable; additional senses appended rather than spawning duplicate cards (v1 default).
  - Huge unique-word counts in books → threshold + batches + coverage indicator; KJV archaic function words excluded as noise.
  - Hour-long audio: upload size limits, chunking, partial-transcription resume; Meet recordings that arrive as video containers → accept and extract the audio track (free OSS, e.g., ffmpeg) rather than rejecting.
  - Chat tool-calls that fail mid-add → surfaced in-thread, never a silent no-op.
  - Suggestions exhausting the sensible pool at a level → say so; never pad with junk or repeats.
  - Mochi duplicates and malformed rows → import report, nothing dropped silently.
  - Phone offline / server unreachable → clear, plain-language error; no fake offline mode.
  - Gutenberg fetch failures / odd encodings → retryable job with visible error.
- **Failure modes / graceful degradation:**
  - LLM or transcription provider down or rate-limited: reviews and quizzes keep working from cached content; ingestion/transcription/generation jobs queue, retry with backoff, and surface status. User input — including uploaded audio — is never lost.
  - Job crashes mid-book or mid-lesson: job state persists; resumes from last completed chunk.
  - DB write failure: fail loudly in UI + logs; never pretend success.
