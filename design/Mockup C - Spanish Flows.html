Después de tantos años fuera, sentía una profunda _____ por su tierra.

Complete the sentence.

Why

Añoranza is the melancholy longing for a place or time one is away from — exactly what years abroad produce. Vergüenza (shame) and desidia (apathy) don’t fit “por su tierra,” and soltura (ease, fluency) is a positive trait, not a feeling directed at home.

Correct.

Subjuntivo: disparadores de emoción

READ ONCE · LAST QUIZ 80% · GENERATED JUN 2

When a main clause expresses an emotion about something — joy, fear, surprise, irritation — the verb in the subordinate clause takes the subjunctive, even when the fact itself is completely real. Spanish marks that you are reacting to the event, not reporting it.

Me alegra que vengas a vernos. — I’m glad you’re coming to see us.

Temía que el tren no llegara a tiempo. — I was afraid the train wouldn’t arrive on time.

The trigger is the emotion verb plus que with a change of subject. If the subject doesn’t change, the infinitive replaces the whole construction — no subjunctive, no que.

Me alegra verte. — same subject: infinitive, not subjunctive.

Watch the tense pairing: a present-tense emotion takes the present subjunctive; a past-tense emotion pulls the imperfect subjunctive. Mixing them (temía que no llegue) is the most common B2-level slip — and the one this topic’s quiz will press on.
